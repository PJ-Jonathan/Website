Data from Hikes and Observations

There are 3 ways to collect and submit data: (See below for details)

  1. Use the NYeBear app: If you have a smartphone, we recommend this. It is easiest and provides us with more accurate and real-time data.
  2. Use paper: If you don't have a smartphone, you may print out a paper version to take with you when you are out. Bring a GPS unit to record your hike as GPS waypoints. Then you can scan it into the computer and send it to us through the website, or snail-mail it to us at:
        ATTN: Cat Sun

        302 Fernow Hall

  3. Using this website: If you did not go on a hike but observed a bear, you can submit it through the app or the online form.
**You should submit observations/data regardless of whether or not you actually saw a bear or signs of bear. All hikes and observations are important ecause they allow researchers to accurately estimate the range of black bears in New York. **

Using NYeBear App

  1. When you start a hike, turn ON the "Start a Hike"/ "Track Hiking" function.
    • It is never too late to start a hike, even if you forgot to do this until you had an observation to report!
    • If you did not go on a hike and are reporting a standalone observation, skip this step.
  2. When/if you observe a bear or signs of bear (hair/scat/pawprint) go to "Report an Observation"
  3. Fill out the requested information:
    1. Use the "Use current location" button to automatically fill the fields with your current location, and should only be used if you are actually where you made your observation.
    2. Check all the signs that you observed.
    3. Rate how confident you are about the sign being from black bear: 1=low to 5=high
    4. We recommend attaching photo(s) of your bear observation or sign.
    5. Provide any other comments about your observation.
    6. Submit! Congratulations! :)
  4. When you finish your hike, turn OFF the "Start a Hike"/"Track Hiking" function.


Using the Paper version

  1. Download/print the Hike Datasheet. When you go on a hike, bring the datasheet and a GPS Unit to record locations/waypoints.
  2. Before you hit the trail/hike, fill out "Before the hike" and "Citizen Scientist Information" on the datasheet.
  3. When you start your hike, record the start time and GPS coordinates on the first row.
  4. At regular intervals (we suggest choosing either 5,10, or 15 minutes;more frequent is better but no more than 15 minutes),
    • record the time,
    • location,
    • any bear sign you may have observed,
    • how confident you are in those signs being from black bear.
  5. When you are finished, provide any comments or thoughts about your hike or any bear signs or observations,
  6. Fill out "After the hike" at the top of the datasheet.
  7. Mail the form back to us, or submit it online by following the instructions for Using this website

Using this Website, nyebear.org

  1. Choose "Submit an Observation" on the Quick Submit page.
  2. Fill out your email so that we can contact you in case you/we have questions.
  3. Then, provide the date and start time of your hike or observation.
  4. Fill out the remaining data.
  5. Find the latitude and longitude of your observation. If you are submitting an entire hike, use the starting location of your hike.
    1. For example, use Google Maps in a new window to find the location;
    2. Right click and select "What's Here";
    3. A dialogue box under the search bar appears with latitude and longitude;
    4. Copy and past into the text fields.
  6. We recommend you attach photo(s) of your bear observation or sign.
    • If you are submitting an entire hike, upload a scanned copy of your datasheet.
  7. Provide any other comments about your observation.
  8. Submit! Congratulations!


Data from Trail Cameras

*If you have a camera out for an entire season, we recommend you submit data regularly and frequently,~once a week or biweekly**
  1. Choose "Submit Trail Camera Data" on the Quick Submit page.
  2. Fill out your email so that we can contact you in case you/we have questions.
  3. Then, provide the dates and times of the camera period you are reporting.
  4. Fill out the remaining data about your camera.
  5. Find the latitude and longitude of your observation.
    1. For example, use Google Maps in a new window;
    2. Find the camera location:
    3. Right click and select "What's Here";
    4. A dialogue box under the search bar appears with latitude and longitude;
    5. Copy and past into the text fields.
  6. If you caught bears on your camera, report them as separate occurrences.
    • Count it as 1 occurrence if bears appeared multipled times within a 30 minute time block.
  7. Upload us your photos of the bears. Submit photos of other wildlife too!
  8. Provide any comments about the bears or the camera. Did you see other wildlife?
  9. Submit! Congratulations!
