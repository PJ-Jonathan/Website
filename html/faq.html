▼ What's the point of NYeBear?
The point of NYeBear is to collect lots of information about where bears are across New York (NY). Researchers can't be everywhere, so citizens (you!) send us information about their hikes and trail cameras about when you see and dont see bears.

▼ I want to help out! What kind of data does NYeBear collect?
  1. Go on hikes! Tell us about the location, time and duration, and route of your hike, and if you saw bears or bear signs, where and when.
  2. Set a trail camera! Tell us about the location of your camera, details about the brand and model, and whether your saw bears.
    When you see bears or bear signs (hair, scat, pawprint), this is called a "detection".
    When you dont see antyhing, it is called a "non-detection".
    Both kinds of data are important, so be sure you report everything !
    (We wont share your contact, location, or other personal information!)

▼ I think I saw a bear or bear sign. I'm not sure. What do I do?
Awesome!
  • Check out Recognizing Bear Signs . There are also links to other resources.
  • When you submit your data, rate your confidence.
  • Include a picture of it with something like your hand or a coin for scale.
  • You can always contact the researchers with a description and photos.
       If we have questions about the data, we'll email you!

▼ How long is NYeBear?
NYeBear is year round (but bears den in the winter) and will run about 4 years total. We encourage citizen scientists to participate for as long as possible, since this provides long-term continuous data.

2015 is the first year of the project, and it is a pilot year to finetune the project. If you have comments or suggestions, send them our way (contact us)!

▼ So how many bears are in New York?
We don't really know. The New York State Department of Environmental Conservation estimates about 6,000-8,000 : ~ 3,200 in the Adirondacks; ~ 1,800 in the Catskill region; and ~ 1,000 in central-western NY. An objective of NYeBear is to help the NYSDEC refine their estimates of population size and distribution of bears in the state.


▼ I've heard there are more bears in New York now. Is that true? Why?
Successful black bear management and more habitat that bears use. In the 1800s, bears in NY were hunted heavily and they lost a lot of forest to farms. In 1903, NY started protecting bears and regulating hunting. Now, bears are back! As the population continues to grow, the ranges have been expanding, especially into central NY. An objective of NYeBear is to help ascertain and refine the range description of bears in the state.

▼ What is citizen science? Why use it to study black bears in New York ?
Citizen science opens up research to everyone. Citizens can help form research questions and hypotheses, collect and analzye data, and share the findings. It is a great way to collect large amounts of data, over wide regions and long time periods. NYeBear depends on citizens to help collect data about bears because ~89% of land in NY is private - land that researchers would have difficulty getting data from.
