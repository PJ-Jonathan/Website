Citizen scientsts can collect data for NYeBear in 3 ways:

  • The first way is to go on hikes and then report information about it: date, route, any observations of bears, etc. We recommend that citizen scientists repeat their hikes and trails every few weeks to collect data for the same places.
  • A second way is to set a trail camera on your property and routinely check and report photos from it.
  • The third way is to monitor specific research sites for black bear hair. We are still developing this - stay tuned!



Submit data regardless of whether or not you actually saw a bear or bear signs.
 All data - even when bears/signs are not observed - help accurately estimate the bear range in New York.
   All data are solely for research purposes, and kept confidential and secure

 

Data from Hikes/Observations

There are 3 ways to collect and submit data from hikes/observations: (Click on each for detailed instructions)

1) Using the NYeBear App: We recommend this method if you have a smartphone. It is easiest and provides us with more accurate and real-time data.▼
  1. When you start a hike, turn ON the "Start a Hike"/ "Track Hiking" function.
    • It is never too late to start a hike, even if you forgot to do this until you had an observation to report!
    • If you did not go on a hike and are reporting a standalone observation, skip this step.
  2. When/if you observe a bear or signs of bear (hair/scat/pawprint) go to "Report Data" and choose "Report an Observation".
  3. Fill out the requested information:
    1. "Use current location" automatically fills the fields with your current location, and should only be used if you are actually where you made your observation.
    2. Check all the signs that you observed.
    3. Rate how confident you are about the sign being from black bear: 1=low to 5=high
    4. We recommend attaching photo(s) of your bear observation or sign.
    5. Provide any other comments about your observation.
    6. Submit! Congratulations!
  4. When you finish your hike, turn OFF the "Start a Hike"/"Track Hiking" function.

  1. Download/print the Hike Datasheet. When you go on a hike, bring the datasheet and a GPS Unit to record locations/waypoints.

3) Using the Quick Submit on this website:You can submit your hike/observations through the Quick Submit forms ▼

  1. On the Quick Submit page, choose "Submit an Observation" .
  2. Fill out your email so that we can contact you in case you/we have questions.
  3. Then, provide the date and start time of your hike or observation.
  4. Fill out the remaining data.
    • If you are submitting a paper data sheet, still fill out this information as best as possible
  5. Find the latitude and longitude of your observation. If you are submitting an entire hike, use the starting location of your hike. **We are working on a way to submit entire hikes - stay tuned.**
    • With Google Maps, move the pin to your location and zoom in as close as possible;
    • The fields for latitude and longitude will automatically fill in;
    • If you manually enter the latitude and longitude, the map will find verify that location.
  6. We recommend you attach photo(s) of your bear observation or sign.
    • If you are submitting an entire hike, upload a scanned copy of your datasheet.
  7. Provide any other comments about your observation.
  8. Submit! Congratulations!




Data from Trail Cameras

*If you have a camera out for an entire season, we recommend you submit data regularly and frequently: once a week or biweekly**
  1. Choose "Submit Trail Camera Data" on the Quick Submit page.
  2. Fill out your email so that we can contact you in case you/we have questions.
  3. Then, provide the dates and times of the camera period you are reporting.
  4. Fill out the remaining data about your camera.
  5. Find the latitude and longitude of your camera.
    • With Google Maps, move the pin to your location and zoom in as close as possible;
    • The fields for latitude and longitude will automatically fill in;
    • If you manually enter the latitude and longitude, the map will find verify that location.
  6. If you caught bears on your camera, report them as separate occurrences.
    • Count it as 1 occurrence if bears appeared multipled times within a 30 minute time block.
  7. Upload us your photos of the bears. Submit photos of other wildlife too!
  8. Provide any comments about the camera, bears or other wildlife.
  9. Submit! Congratulations!
