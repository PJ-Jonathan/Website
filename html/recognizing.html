Black bears (Ursus americanus) are the only wild species of bear in the eastern United States.

Keep your senses alert as you are hiking and collecting data.

Do not attempt to approach, feed, or harass wildlife.


Familiarize yourself with black bear signs, and what to do if you come across a bear in the wild:



How to identify black bear signs

Black Bear
Other Animals

 Animal   

 Black body with brown/tan  muzzle; brown/cinnamon phases  uncommon in NY.

  Not easily confused.

Small, round ears;occasionally a white patch/"blaze" on chest.

 Variable size. No other wildlife  species are bear-sized and also  black.

 Adults are 3-6' in length from  nose to tail; short tail (3-5"); 100- 500 lbs.

 Coyotes, wolves, dogs have  longer tails and pointed ears

  Hair   

 Approx. 1-4" long; guard hairs  are all black; underfur may be  brown.

 Raccoon, oppossum, and deer  hair have multiple other colors  (white, cream, tan) on a strand.

 Shed hair may be lighter from  sun exposure

 Black hair from skunk is fine,  usually longer than 5"

 Hair is typically found on ground, tree trunks, barbed wired

 Tracks   

 Broad footprints, about 4-7" long,  with 5 toes and sometimes claw  marks

 Human tracks are oblong/oval.  Bear hind tracks are triangular.
 Tracks are typically found on trails, in soft soil, mud, or sand

 Scat

 Usually dark brown (varies by  diet), ~1-2" diameter; usually  tubular and sometimes coiled

 Deer scat may be similar in  overall size but is usually of  clumped, smaller pellets

 If diet was mainly berries, may be  soft and liquidy

 Dog scat may be similar in shape  but smaller in size

 Contents may include grasses,  seeds, animal hair, shells

 Scat can be found anywhere, but typically on trails, sides of trails, at tree/plant bases


What to do if you see a black bear:

(adapted from the book, "Living With Bears" by Linda Masterson and the Be Bear Aware Program)

  1. Stay calm.
  2. Do not run.
  3. Avoid eye contact with the bear.
  4. Facing the bear, back away slowly.
  5. If the bear approaches, make noise - scream, yell, blow your whistle.
  6. Do not leave food out in the open.
  7. Consider carrying bear spray and make sure you know how use it properly.
