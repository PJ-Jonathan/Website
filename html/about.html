NYeBear studies black bears by collecting data from citizen scientists across New York



Get involved and join NYeBear!

  • Dowload the NYeBear app
  • Go on hikes
  • Set up a personal trail camera
  • Submit observations of bears and bear signs

Citizen scientists can play a big role in helping researchers answer questions like, "How many bears are there?" and "Where are they?". Approximately ~ 89% of NY land is privately owned - areas that can be difficult for researchers to access and study.

With NYeBear, citizen scientists submit information about where and when they detect (or not!) bears and bear signs. This data across NY helps estimate population sizes and distributions.

Black bears are an important wildlife species in NY. Many people enjoy seeing bears and bear sign in the wild. Maintaining a bear population that is healthy and safe for baears an people is an important management objective.

For more information about black bears in New York, visit our FAQ page and the New York State Department of Environmental Conservation's website.
