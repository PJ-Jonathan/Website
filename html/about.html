NYeBear is a citizen science project in New York where citizens submit information from 1) hikes, 2) trail cameras, and 3) research sites about where they see (and don’t see!) signs of black bears. It is accessible by website and smartphone via an app. This data goes towards helping researchers monitor the black bear population. The more people who participate in NYeBear and submit information, the more accurately researchers can determine the population size, range, and other important aspects of black bear ecology in New York.

Black bears are an important wildlife species in New York. They are one of our largest mammals in the state, and many people enjoy seeing bears and bear sign in the wild. However, the number and intensity of negative human-bear interactions, including motor vehicle collisions, property and agricultural damage has been increasing because the population has been growing and their range has been expanding (due to conservative management and the reversion of many farmlands back to forest).

Maintaining a population size of bears in the state that is healthy and safe for both bears and human is an important management objective. As the bear population grows and their range expands in New York, researchers need data from all across the state about where there are and aren’t bears. Approximately 89% of land in New York is privately owned, so researchers need the help of citizens to do this.

For more information about black bears in New York, you can visit the New York State Department of Environmental Conservation’s website .
