About the creators of NY eBear

Cat Sun is a graduate student in the Department of Natural at Cornell University and New York Cooperative Fish and Wildlife Research Unit. She studies black bears in New York to better understand their population size, distribution across the state, and patterns of resource use. To make NYeBear, she is collaborating with PJ Finlay and Jonathan Gomes Selman, who are currently students at Ithaca High School. PJ and Jonathan have developed the iOS and Android apps for NYeBear and also are the brains behind managing all the technology behind the data collection.

NYeBear is a citizen science project in New York that collects data on the presence and absence of black bears across the state.

Black bears are an important wildlife species in NY. They are one of our largest mammals in the state, and many people enjoy seeing bears and bear sign in the wild. The population of bear in NY has been growing since the 1950s due to successful management and the reversion of many farmlands back to forest.

Maintaining a population of bears in the state that is healthy and safe for both bears and people is an important management objective. Researchers ask, "How many bears are there? Where are they?". Citizen science is a way to collect data from all across the state about where there are and aren't bears, since ~ 89% of land in NY is privately owned.

You can be a citizen scientist and help black bear research! If you spend time outdoors in NY, join NYeBear. You can submit information from hikes and personal trail cameras about when and where you see (and don't see!) bear or bear signs. Learn about bear signs here. The more people who are part of NYeBear and submit information, the more it helps researchers determine the population size, range, and other important aspects of black bear ecology in NY.

For more information about black bears in New York, visit the New York State Department of Environmental Conservation's website.
