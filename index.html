Collecting black bear population data from citizen scientists in New York.

NYeBear is a citizen science project in New York where citizens submit information from hikes, trail cameras, and research sites about where and when they see (and donâ€™t see!) signs of black bears. Approximately 89% of land in New York is privately owned, so researchers need the help of citizens. These data goes towards helping researchers monitor the black bear population. The more people who participate and submit information, the more accurately researchers can determine the population size, range, and other important aspects of black bear ecology in New York.



How to Contribute

If you are interested there are three different ways to contribute to our study. The first is to report observations, or lack of observations of bears and their signs. If you have seen a bear, bear hair, bear scat, or have gone to a wooded area and did not see a bear then please submit observation data. The second way to submit is if you have a trail camera set up in New York. It is very useful to us if you tell us when your trail camera has been operational even if it has not seen any black bears. The final submission type is to routinely check a bear hair trap that we set up. If you are interested in this please contact us in the contact tab.
