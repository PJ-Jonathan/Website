Welcome to NY eBear!


Do you live, hike, or spend time outdoors in New York? Ever wonder if there are bears where you live? Become a NYeBear citizen scientist and join researchers at Cornell University studying black bears in New York!

We are looking for people to be citizen scientists who will record data about the presence and absence of bears and bear signs when they 1) go on hikes with their smartphones or GPS units, and/or 2) set out personal trail cameras on their property.

The number of bears in the state has been growing markedly since the 1990's. More bears can mean more viewing opportunities but also wildlife conflicts. Researchers need to estimate how many bears there are and where they are in order to help wildlife management.

Explore the website to learn more and to submit your NYeBear data.

Stay tuned as we add spotlights on citizen scientists and interactive maps as data come in showing where bears have been seen! Check back soon!
